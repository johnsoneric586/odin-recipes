Cozy Chicken and Dumplings

Description

Doesn't that picture just scream cozy? This southern-inspired chicken and dumplings recipe will leave your family speechless! This recipe is made from scratch and uses techniques to ensure the juiciest chicken, most flavorful homemade soup, and airy, fluffy dumplings that steam to perfection.

Chicken and Dumplings start with a savory chicken soup with vegetables in a creamy broth. The soup is topped with uncooked dumplings that are then steamed on top. It's a popular comfort food in the Southern US, and can vary in terms of how thick the soup is and what kind of dumplings are used.

Ingredients

Main

  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 2 lbs. of bone-in skinless chicken breast or thigh
  • Salt/pepper to taste
  • 5 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 small yellow onion
  • 1 cup of carrots, diced
  • 2 sticks of celery, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of hot sauce
  • 1/3 cup of flour
  • 41/2 cups of chicken broth
  • 11/2 cups of half and half
  • 3/4 cups of frozen peas

Seasonings

  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon EACH: dried basil, parsely, thyme, rosemary, mustard powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground sage
  • 1/4 teaspoon of pepper

Dumplings

  • 2 cups of cake flour, or regular flour
  • 2 teapsoons of baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 2 teapsoons of sugar
  • 3/4 cup of cold sour cream
  • 1/4 cup of cold milk
  • 4 tablespoons of butter, melted

Steps

Sear the chicken

  1. Season each side of the chicken with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a 4 ½-quart soup pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chicken and cook for 3 minutes per side, until a little bit of a brown color has developed. The middle will still be uncooked.
  4. Let the chicken rest for 10 minutes, then dice into bite-sized pieces.

Make the Soup

  1. Combine the seasonings and set aside.
  2. Melt the butter in the same pot over medium heat and use a silicone spatula to “clean” the bottom of the pot.
  3. Add the diced onions, carrots, and celery and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the minced garlic, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, and seasonings. Cook for 1 more minute.
  5. Add the flour and toss to coat. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring continuously.
  6. Add the chicken broth in small splashes, stirring continuously. Scrape up any remnants from the bottom of the pot as you do so.
  7. Add the half and half in the same manner.
  8. Add the chicken bouillon, if using.
  9. Add frozen peas.
  10. Bring to a gentle boil and let it simmer, uncovered, while you make the dumplings.

Make the Dumplings

  1. Combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, garlic powder, and sugar in a medium bowl.
  2. Add the milk, sour cream, and melted butter. Use a folding motion to combine and form a dough, but don't overmix or they will be dense.
  3. Add the chicken back to the soup along with any juices from the plate. Stir to combine and reduce heat to low.
  4. (If using leftover chicken, add it now.)
  5. Use a small cookie scoop and carefully place the dumplings over the soup in an even layer. Spoon a little liquid over each one.
  6. Cover tightly and increase heat slightly to bring it to a gentle simmer. Set a timer for 15 minutes, don’t lift the lid during this time, the dumplings need to steam.
  7. Open the lid and insert a toothpick into the middle of a dumpling to check for doneness. If it comes out clean, it’s ready. If not, cover and simmer for a few more minutes.
  8. Once the middle is set, garnish with parsley and serve!
